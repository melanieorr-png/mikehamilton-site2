National Skills Agreement (NSA): VET Funding, RTO Strategy & Delivery

The National Skills Agreement (NSA) is reshaping how vocational education and training is funded and delivered across Australia. For Registered Training Organisations, businesses and government partners, success under the NSA requires more than compliance — it requires strategic alignment, strong positioning and the ability to deliver real workforce outcomes.

Proven delivery across the Northern Territory and remote Australia — where complexity, remoteness and workforce challenges demand a different level of expertise.

What is the National Skills Agreement?

The National Skills Agreement is a funding framework between the Australian Government and state and territory governments that sets the direction for vocational education and training delivery, investment and reform.

It focuses on priority industries, workforce demand and improved outcomes for learners, while strengthening accountability and performance expectations across the VET system.

Why the NSA Matters for RTOs and Businesses

  • Directly influences access to government funding
  • Shapes training priorities and delivery expectations
  • Determines competitiveness in tender and contract processes
  • Requires alignment with workforce and industry demand

How Organisations Access VET Funding Under the NSA

Funding is typically accessed through state and territory training contracts, competitive tenders and alignment with government priorities such as targeted workforce initiatives.

Success requires strong positioning, clear alignment to industry demand, and the ability to demonstrate delivery capability — particularly in regional and remote environments.

Common Challenges Organisations Face

  • Misalignment with National Skills Agreement priorities
  • Weak or generic tender and grant responses
  • Limited understanding of jurisdictional funding requirements
  • Difficulty delivering in regional, remote or complex environments
  • Underdeveloped commercial and funding strategies
