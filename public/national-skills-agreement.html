What is the National Skills Agreement?

The National Skills Agreement (NSA) is the Australian Government’s major national framework for vocational education and training reform. It commenced on 1 January 2024 as a 5-year agreement between the Commonwealth and states and territories to strengthen the VET sector.

Why the NSA Matters for RTOs and Businesses

The NSA shapes how funding, reform priorities and performance expectations flow through the VET system. It highlights priorities including Closing the Gap, TAFE Centres of Excellence, foundation skills, digital capability, housing supply, and essential care services.

  • Access to government funding opportunities
  • Alignment with national workforce priorities
  • Support for Closing the Gap outcomes
  • Links to TAFE Centres of Excellence
  • Stronger expectations around quality and accountability

Why the Northern Territory is Different

Delivering under the National Skills Agreement in the Northern Territory is fundamentally different to metropolitan environments. Remote logistics, workforce mobility, cultural capability and thin markets all shape what effective delivery looks like.
