What is the National Skills Agreement?

The National Skills Agreement (NSA) is the Australian Government’s primary funding framework for vocational education and training (VET). It sets national priorities across workforce development, training delivery, and system reform.

Why the NSA Matters

  • Access to major government funding streams
  • Alignment with national workforce priorities
  • Closing the Gap commitments
  • Centres of Excellence and priority sectors
  • Increased performance and accountability expectations

Why the Northern Territory is Different

Delivering under the National Skills Agreement in the Northern Territory is fundamentally different to metropolitan environments.

  • Remote and regional delivery complexity
  • Workforce mobility and access challenges
  • Culturally responsive training requirements
  • Thin markets and service limitations
